Lifestyle Fit

Match energy levels: active breeds need engaged owners, calm breeds suit relaxed homes. Cats live 12-20 years -- choose for the long haul.

Financial Commitment

$500-2,000+/yr for food, litter, vet care. Purebreds: $500-3,000 upfront. Pet insurance: $15-40/mo. Budget for emergencies.

Space & Environment

Most cats adapt to apartments. Active breeds need vertical space (trees, shelves). Cat-proof your home -- secure windows, remove toxic plants.

Time & Attention

30-60 min play daily. Social breeds hate being alone -- consider a pair if you work long hours. Add 15-30 min for feeding, grooming, litter.

Family & Children

Some breeds are kid-friendly, others prefer adults. Check children-friendly ratings. Supervise all cat-child interactions.

Other Pets

Choose pet-friendly breeds if you have other animals. Introduce gradually over 1-2 weeks with scent swapping.

Grooming & Shedding

Long-hair: daily brush + pro grooming ($30-80/session). Heavy shedders need frequent vacuuming. Higher grooming rating = less effort.

Health & Lifespan

Research breed-specific conditions. Choose a breeder who provides health screenings. Regular vet care and quality diet extend lifespan.

Adoption vs. Breeder

Shelters: $50-200, includes vaccines and spay/neuter. Breeders: predictable temperament, higher cost. Avoid online-only sellers.

Preparation Checklist

  • Ceramic or steel food/water bowls
  • Quality cat food (age-appropriate)
  • Litter box + unscented clumping litter
  • Scratching post or cat tree
  • Comfortable bed in a quiet spot
  • Carrier for vet visits
  • Wand, ball, and puzzle toys
  • Water fountain (encourages drinking)
  • Nail clippers + grooming brush
  • Vet appointment within first week
